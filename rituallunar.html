Lunar Diamond Rituals

The moon is the keeper of tides and the silent guardian of intuition. To walk the Lunar Diamond path is to learn how to read the moon’s phases, for she governs water, emotions, and the rhythm of our inner world. Whether you are a beginner or advanced practitioner, understanding the moon simplifies readings and deepens your connection to the unseen.

Rituals begin with preparation. Choose the day of the week that resonates most with you — some days carry stronger energies than others. Consult the Aurora Calendar to align with celestial events: showers of meteors on March 10th and 15th, or the lunar eclipse on the 20th, magnify the witch’s work.

Prepare your space: lay out cloth, tools, candles, statues, and a rug. Invite your familiar — the cat — into the environment at least one hour before the ritual. Cleanse the cat gently with a handkerchief, visualizing the removal of all stagnant energy. Listen to the rhythm of its breath, the soothing purrs, and connect your mind with the feeling you wish to manifest. In this shared atmosphere, balance begins to take shape.

🌙 Rituals for Beginners (5–20 minutes)

These rituals are designed for beginners, each lasting between 5 and 20 minutes. They align with lunar phases, days of the week, and times of day, while weaving in the protective energy of the cat as familiar and the wisdom of the Maneki Neko cards.

Love Ritual (Waxing Moon, Friday sunset)

  • Moon phase: Waxing Moon
  • Day/Time: Friday at sunset
  • Action: Light a pink candle, visualize union.
  • Cat energy: Feel your cat’s breathing as a bridge to the heart.

Begin by shuffling your Maneki Neko cards. Draw one and let its message guide your heart — perhaps it speaks of attraction, perhaps of harmony.

Light a pink candle slowly, watching the flame rise. Whisper the name of your beloved or the qualities of love you wish to invite. Speak aloud: “I call love that is true, gentle, and enduring.”

Sit with your cat. Place your hand near its chest, feel the rhythm of its breath. Imagine this rhythm carrying your words into the unseen. Close by thanking the Waxing Moon for growing your desire, and your cat for guarding your heart’s wish.

Career Ritual (New Moon, Monday morning)

  • Moon phase: New Moon
  • Day/Time: Monday morning
  • Action: Write a career goal on paper, place it under the Tarot Magician card.
  • Cat energy: See your cat’s gaze as guidance toward opportunity.

Draw a Maneki Neko card. Let its symbol show you the energy of opportunity.

Write a career goal on paper, fold it, and place it beneath the Tarot Magician card. As you do, say: “I manifest skill, focus, and success.”

Sit beside your cat. Watch its gaze — steady, patient, wise. Imagine that gaze guiding you toward unseen doors. The New Moon is the seed of beginnings; trust that your intention will grow.

Final Reflection: The Moon as Tarot of the Sky

Each lunar phase opens a different portal: the New Moon plants, the Waxing Moon builds, the Full Moon illuminates, and the Waning Moon cleanses. But these are not just cycles in the sky — they are mirrors of your own inner rhythm. When you light a candle, whisper a name, or plant a seed, you are not performing something external; you are aligning your heartbeat with the pulse of the cosmos.

The cat, as familiar and protector, reminds us that magic is not distant or abstract. It is felt in everyday gestures — in breath, in silence, in presence. When you place your hand on your cat’s chest and feel its breathing, you are touching the rhythm of the unseen. In folklore, the Maneki Neko raises its paw to invite fortune and protection; in ritual, your cat raises its spirit to guard your path.

Tarot language teaches us that every card is a doorway. The Moon card whispers of intuition, the Magician of creation, the Empress of abundance. When paired with lunar phases, these archetypes become living companions: the New Moon as the Fool’s first step, the Waxing Moon as the Magician’s will, the Full Moon as the High Priestess’ revelation, the Waning Moon as Death’s release.

To learn the Moon is to learn the Tarot written across the sky. To honor the cat is to honor the guardian who walks beside you in both worlds. And to practice these rituals — even for five minutes — is to remember that you are part of a greater cycle, a dance of light and shadow, of planting and letting go, of breathing and becoming.
